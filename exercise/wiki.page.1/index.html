Crown Shyness

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

For the album by Trash Boat, see Crown Shyness (album)

Crown Shyness (also canopy disengagement, [1] canopy shyness,[2] or inter-crown spacing [3] is a phenomenon observed in some tree species, in which the crown of full stocked tree do not touch each other, forming a canopy with change like gaps. [4][5] the phenomenon is the most prevelant among trees of the same species, but also occurs between tree of different species.[6][7] there exist many hypothesis as to why crown shyness is adaptive behavior , and research suggest that might inhibit spread of the leaf eating larvae.[8]

Possible physiological explanations

the exact physiological basis of crown is not certain [6] the phenomenon has been discussed in scientific literture since the 1920s.[9] the variety of hypothesis and expermental results suggest that there are multiple mechanisms across difference species, and example of convergent evolution.[citation needed]
